Bisi Adjapon

Has taken possession of my entire soul, like these sweet mornings of spring which I enjoy with my whole heart.

Of Women and Frogs

When her curiosity about her body leads to a ginger-in-the-vagina punishment from her stepmother, Esi, a feisty half-Nigerian girl, begins to question the restrictions adults place on girls.

This is a really wonderful story. [Bisi Adjapon] writes with incredible vividness and clarity. [Her] similes and attention to all of the senses are really extraordinary.

Dave Eggers
publisher of McSweeney’s Quarterly Concern and Pulitzer Prize-winning author of A Heartbreaking Work of Staggering Genius

What a lovely  lovely read.  I want to buy copies to gift to every young woman I know. I laughed out loud in places.  I swear it's a universal African girl story

Zukiswa Wanner
winner of the K Sello Duiker Memorial Literary Award for London Cape Town Joburg

I was stealing time to read between patients. Don't remember the last time I did that. An enticing and mesmerizing read. It just pulls you along, and tugs at your heart, bringing back long forgotten strong and confusing feelings. A work of great courage and commitment. We’ll done!

Doctor Afuah Laing
Gynecologist and founder of Obaatan Pa Free Clinic for Women.

Getting the feel of a place right in fiction is a tough thing to do.   Of Women and Frogs does not disappoint. Bisi Adjapon has addressed some of the truly  difficult aspects of love and sexuality for girls and women in Ghana fearlessly and skillfully. 5 stars.
